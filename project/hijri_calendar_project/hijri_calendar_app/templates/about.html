Purpose

The website uses data obtained from official "pakmoonsighting.pk" and makes it available as a public API.

Background

Pakistan launched its first ever moon-sighting website on May 28th 2019 in a bid to end decades-old controversy over the start of key lunar months that decides the beginning of the holy month of Ramazan and Eid festivals.

The website "pakmoonsighting.pk" was inaugurated on Sunday, two weeks after Federal Minister for Science and Technology Fawad Chaudhry made an announcement regarding the preparation of a scientific lunar calendar.

"This is the first official website of the country which marks the important religious festivals for the next five years, Fawad Hussain Chaudhry, the minister of science and technology, told reporters in Islamabad."

The move aims to end controversy surrounding moon-sighting in the country with various clerics fighting for relevance.

Technologies

  1. Python with Django 2.2, and DRF (Django rest framework)
  2. Postgres database
  3. Redis cache
  4. Containerization via Docker and Docker-Compose
  5. Hosting on Microsoft Azure Cloud platform
  6. Google Material Design
